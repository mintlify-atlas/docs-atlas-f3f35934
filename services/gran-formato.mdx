Large-format printing is the backbone of visual communication at events, trade shows, retail environments, and public spaces. At Reprodisseny, we produce everything from single roll-up banners to multi-panel exhibition systems and outdoor signage — with high color fidelity and durable materials suited to the exposure conditions.

## Typical products

- Roll-up banners and retractable displays
- Backlit and non-backlit banners (lona, textil, mesh)
- Step-and-repeat backdrops for events and press areas
- Floor graphics and aisle markers
- Outdoor signage and hoarding panels
- Hanging banners and ceiling mobiles
- Rigid board displays (foam board, PVC, aluminum composite)
- Window graphics and perforated vinyl

## Production equipment

All large-format work is produced on **HP latex plotters**, which use water-based latex inks. These inks are safe for indoor environments, produce vivid colors, and are resistant to scratching and UV exposure. For rigid substrates, we use the **HP R1000**, which prints directly onto boards up to 2.5m wide.

Cutting and finishing is handled on a **Kongsberg digital cutting table**, which enables precise die-cutting, kiss-cutting, and routing of complex shapes without manual error.

<Info>
  HP latex inks are water-based and do not emit harmful VOCs, making them safe for installation in hospitals, schools, and enclosed spaces.
</Info>

## File requirements for large format

Large-format files have different resolution requirements than small-format print. Because viewing distances are greater, effective resolution can be lower:

| Viewing distance | Recommended resolution |
|---|---|
| &lt; 1 m (close inspection) | 150–200 ppi at print size |
| 1–3 m (typical trade show) | 100–150 ppi at print size |
| &gt; 3 m (outdoor / building) | 72–100 ppi at print size |

<Warning>
  Always specify the **final print size** in your file or brief. Do not scale up a small-format file — artifacts will be visible even at distance.
</Warning>

- **Color mode**: CMYK
- **Format**: PDF or high-resolution TIFF/JPEG
- **Bleed**: 10–30mm depending on finishing (confirm with our team)
- **Fonts**: Convert to outlines before export
- **Images**: Embed all linked images in the final file
